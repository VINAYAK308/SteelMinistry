Green Steel Initiative

The Green Steel Initiative by the Ministry of Steel is a comprehensive plan aimed at decarbonizing the Indian steel industry and reducing its environmental footprint launched in December 2024.

PLI Scheme Launching

Launched on July 29, 2021 the scheme focuses to boost domestic production of specialty steel grades, which are high-value and have applications in various sectors like defense, automobiles, and electrical appliances. This reduces reliance on imports and promotes "Atmanirbhar Bharat" (self-reliant India).

Industrial Incentives: Speciality Steel




Transforming industries
with innovative efficient solutions

The primary incentive for specialty steel production in India comes through the Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme for Specialty Steel by Ministry. Financial Incentives: The core of the PLI scheme is direct financial support. Companies that meet specific criteria for increased production and sales of eligible specialty steel grades receive incentives.

Modernization of Steel Plants

The Ministry is supporting and encouraging pilot projects to demonstrate the feasibility and effectiveness of CCUS technologies in Indian steel plants. This includes initiatives like: Tata Steel's 5 TPD CO2 capture plant at Jamshedpur. JSW's 100 TPD CO2 capture unit at their DRI plant in Salav. JSPL's 3000 TPD capacity Carbon Capturing Unit at Angul, Odisha.
